SEDANS

Choose a sedan for its suitability and economical factors. Sedans are described as having "three-box" bodies: the front box is home to the engine; the middle box is larger and houses the passengers; and the third box, located in the rear, is the trunk. These are the classic proportions of a sedan and have been for more than 70 years.




SUVS

Choose a SUV for large family members and enough money to feed them. SUVs typically have high ground clearance and a tall body. This results in a high center of mass, which makes SUVs more prone to roll-over accidents. SUVs were quoted as 2.5 times more likely to roll over in a crash than regular cars and that SUV roofs.
